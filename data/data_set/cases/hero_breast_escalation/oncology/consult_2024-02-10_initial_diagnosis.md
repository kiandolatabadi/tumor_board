# Oncology Consultation -- Initial Diagnosis
**Date:** 2024-02-10 | **Provider:** Dr. Anika Patel, Medical Oncology

**Reason for visit:** New diagnosis, left breast mass.

**History:** 32-year-old woman presents after core biopsy of a palpable left breast mass confirms invasive ductal carcinoma. Staging workup (mammogram, breast MRI, CT chest/abdomen/pelvis) shows no distant disease. Clinical stage IIB (cT2N1M0).

**Biomarkers:** ER 90%, PR 60%, HER2 IHC 1+ (not amplified), Ki-67 28%, grade 2.

**Plan:** Upfront lumpectomy and sentinel node biopsy, followed by adjuvant AC-T chemotherapy, adjuvant radiation, and adjuvant endocrine therapy (tamoxifen; patient is premenopausal).

**Fertility and family planning:** Patient and her husband raised the topic of future pregnancy during today's visit. Given the need to begin chemotherapy promptly, the fertility preservation conversation was deferred; plan documented to revisit once treatment response is established and a disease-free interval is confirmed. No referral to reproductive endocrinology placed at this time.

**Goals of care:** Treatment intent is curative. Patient's goal is to complete curative-intent therapy and return to full function; this framework is documented as the working goals-of-care basis for this phase of treatment.
